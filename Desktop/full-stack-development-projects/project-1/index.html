Learn about the Hungarian revolution of 1956

In October 1956, Hungarian students and workers rose up against Soviet control, demanding freedom, democracy, and the withdrawal of Soviet troops. What began as a peaceful protest soon escalated into a nationwide uprising that shook the Communist bloc and inspired movements across Europe. Though the revolution was crushed within weeks, its legacy lived on as a symbol of resistance and national pride.
